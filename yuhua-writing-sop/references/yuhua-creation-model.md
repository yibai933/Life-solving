# Yu Hua Creation Model

## Corpus Map

The local collected works used for this model include:

- Long novels: `活着`, `兄弟`, `许三观卖血记`, `在细雨中呼喊`
- Short and mid-length fiction collections: `黄昏里的男孩`, `战栗`, `世事如烟`, `我胆小如鼠`, `鲜血梅花`, `现实一种`
- Essays and writing talks: `没有一条道路是重复的`, `温暖和百感交集的旅程`, `音乐影响了我的写作`

Treat the corpus as three material layers:

- Story layer: fate, family, hunger, blood, death, absurd social change, bodily humiliation.
- Method layer: Yu Hua's ideas about inner truth, literary reality, memory, rhythm, music, and listening to fictional characters.
- Public-topic layer: ordinary people facing systems, speed, money, shame, family obligation, and the comic cruelty of life.

## Content Positioning

Yu Hua's works are useful for articles about how ordinary people survive history, family, poverty, desire, and absurd reality without becoming clean moral symbols. The best articles do not merely say "life is hard"; they show the strange mechanics of hardship:

- A person sells blood to hold up a family.
- A man loses almost everything and still calls it life rather than performance.
- A town becomes a theater where wealth, sex, media, slogans, and vanity expose one another.
- Childhood memory returns not as nostalgia, but as a wound with weather, smell, hunger, and embarrassment.
- Reality becomes literary when an ordinary fact gains emotional pressure and cannot be forgotten.

## Persona Library

Choose one article persona before writing:

- Witness: calm, restrained, lets scenes carry the knife.
- Late-night friend: conversational, warm, understands the reader's fatigue.
- Cold-eyed commentator: sharper, better for social topics, avoids sentimental endings.
- Self-mocking survivor: suitable for workplace, family, debt, and adulthood themes.
- Public-topic essayist: connects literature to contemporary social emotion without becoming a news commentary.

## Core Themes

- Living vs surviving: from outside, survival looks like a statistic; from inside, it is still life.
- Body as currency: blood, labor, hunger, illness, sex, and fatigue become ways reality charges people.
- Family as debt and shelter: family saves people, consumes people, and gives suffering a name.
- Absurd reality: the world often becomes ridiculous exactly when people are most serious.
- Memory and loss: what remains is not complete truth, but the stubborn fragment that refuses to disappear.
- Dignity among the undignified: the person may be ridiculous, poor, compromised, or defeated, but not empty.
- Inner truth against decorative truth: good writing gets close to what people do not want to admit.
- Equality before death and hardship: social rank collapses when the body, hunger, illness, or grief arrives.

## Topic Entry Points

Use these angles to generate topics:

- From one character to one modern dilemma: Fu Gui and endurance, Xu Sanguan and family debt, Li Guangtou and vulgar success.
- From one object to one theme: blood, bowl, road, rain, town, body, bicycle, hospital, music.
- From one contradiction: funny and cruel, alive and emptied, vulgar and sincere, weak and stubborn, poor and generous.
- From one modern sentence: "成年人不是不崩溃，是崩溃也要排队"; then locate its Yu Hua equivalent.
- From one writing method: listen to characters, turn reality into remembered pressure, let a tiny detail pull out a whole life.

## Title Patterns

- Counterintuitive: `余华最狠的地方，不是写苦，是写人怎么把苦过成日子`
- Object-metaphor: `一碗饭、一袋血和一个人不肯倒下的样子`
- Character hook: `许三观不是卖血，他是在给一家人续命`
- Modern connection: `读懂余华后，才知道成年人为什么总在硬撑`
- Sharp claim: `所谓活着，就是把命运给的烂牌一张张打完`
- Gentle irony: `生活没有放过他，他也没打算放过生活`

## Framework Templates

### Template A: Character To Modern Life

1. Open with a contemporary pressure.
2. Introduce one Yu Hua character as a mirror.
3. Explain the visible plot, then the hidden mechanism.
4. Connect to today's reader.
5. Turn: the character is not a moral role model; the character exposes a truth.
6. End with dignity, not inspirational slogan.

### Template B: Theme Dissection

1. Start with a sentence that overturns a common understanding.
2. Define the theme precisely.
3. Use two or three Yu Hua scenes across works.
4. Show the same theme in current life.
5. Give the reader a usable insight.
6. End with a clear emotional lift.

### Template C: Writing Method Extraction

1. Start from a Yu Hua writing idea: inner truth, listening, reality, rhythm, memory.
2. Explain what ordinary writing gets wrong.
3. Show how Yu Hua handles the problem in fiction.
4. Convert it into a practical writing method.
5. Demonstrate with a small example.
6. End by returning to why writing matters.

## Emotional Curves

- Endurance curve: shock -> grief -> numbness -> recognition -> quiet strength.
- Absurd curve: laughter -> discomfort -> ugliness -> truth -> sober humor.
- Family-debt curve: warmth -> burden -> sacrifice -> resentment -> tenderness without purity.
- Memory curve: detail -> recollection -> loss -> understanding -> acceptance.
- Writing-method curve: confusion -> scene -> principle -> method -> creative freedom.

## Material Rules

- Use specific material categories: character, scene, object, bodily action, social setting, author statement.
- Avoid long quotations. Paraphrase scenes and only use short phrases when necessary.
- Pair each literary material with a present-day equivalent.
- Do not overload one article with the whole corpus. Use one main work plus one or two supporting echoes.
- For `活着`, avoid reducing Fu Gui to "positive energy"; emphasize internal narration and the distinction between living and being seen as surviving.
- For `许三观卖血记`, focus on blood as bodily payment for family order, shame, and responsibility.
- For `兄弟`, focus on absurd modernization, vulgar success, media spectacle, and the split between affection and appetite.
- For short stories such as `现实一种`, use them carefully: they carry violence and coldness, suitable for darker essays.
- For essays, use them to explain method: inner truth, literary reality, rhythm, memory, music, and the writer as listener.

## Golden Line Patterns

- `人不是被苦难教育大的，人是被一次次没得选逼大的。`
- `生活最狠的地方，是它从不解释，只收账。`
- `真正的体面，有时不是站得漂亮，而是倒下去之前还记得护住谁。`
- `余华写的不是苦难展览，而是人怎样在烂泥里保留一点人的形状。`
- `成年人所谓坚强，很多时候只是今天还不能散架。`
- `命运没有审美，它给人的东西常常粗糙、难看，还不允许退货。`
- `荒诞不是世界疯了，而是世界一本正经地做着疯狂的事。`
- `一个人最深的尊严，常常藏在他最不体面的时刻。`

Use these as pattern examples. Rewrite them to fit the article instead of copying mechanically.

## Full Article Checklist

Before finalizing:

- Does the article have one clear thesis?
- Is the opening concrete within the first 3 sentences?
- Is every Yu Hua reference doing argumentative work?
- Does the prose avoid school-report tone?
- Does the ending rise without pretending suffering is beautiful?
- Are the best 2-3 sentences placed at emotional turning points rather than stacked at the end?
